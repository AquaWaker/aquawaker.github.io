Brayden Pritchard

Employer: University of Guelph Communications and Public Affairs Division

Position: Web Developer/Data Analyst

Introduction

This webpage is a collection of reflections and summaries of my first coop workterm with the University of Guelph on the Communications and Public Affairs Division as a Web Developer and Data Analyst over the Fall 2021 semester. Presented is information pretaining to my employer, different learning goals I had, and summaries of what I worked on and skills I learned.

Employer Information

This work term I had the pleasure to work with the University's Web Strategy Manager, James Harris, on the Communications and Public Affairs team where I spent my time working on various projects, designing and developing new webpages, or finding existing bugs and fixing them. The team specialized in many other areas other then simply the University's webpage, contributing to different social media platforms, the team promotes the University to new future Guelph Gryphons, and keeps communication with current students about different campus activities, and encouragement during stressful times.

Learning Goals

Communicating - Written Communication

Goal: My first goal for the fall co-op term is to get better at my written communication. I have always found oral communication has come easier to me, written on the other hand I have had to work on. I want to get better at coming to my point, and be able to express the information I need to express in a clear, concise manner.

Action Plan: Considering the collaborative nature of working on a website to the scale of the University of Guelph's site, I will be working on plenty of small projects that will be used by others on the team. This will give me plenty of opportunities to communicate what I have completed or changed. I will use these opportunities to take extra effort to develop my written communications skills.

Measure of Success: I will consider success in developing my skills if I find by the end of the term I do not have to rephrase statements I make in communicating development changes and sharing my efforts in the different tasks I complete.

Reflection: Over the course of this work term I feel I have made some progress in this written communication, I felt like I learned how to come to my point quickly and concisely for quick communication like I had hoped. The way I communicated with my peers though was a lot more casual then I was expecting, so I'm not quite sure if I developed as much of a professional vocabulary as I was expecting. Overall I think my technical language has definitely grown over this term.

Professional & Ethical Behaviour - Teamwork

Goal: My second goal of the work term is to develop better teamwork skills, specifically in how to collaborate on technical tasks. I have great experience from part time jobs that had plenty of opportunities to learn teamwork skills, but now I want to learn how to apply that to the computer science field since school so far has not had a collaborative technical assessment.

Action Plan: Like the first goal, the nature of the job on the website means I will not have to go out of my way to get into situations where communication and teamwork will be important for effectively completing tasks. I will take these situations with the mindset of wanting to develop my skills in teamwork and collaboration on projects.

Measure of Success: I will consider the situations I've been in where communication leads to better productivity and results as a measure of the success I've had. It's hard to quantify the success of developing teamwork from self reflection so it will have to come from what I can defer from the others I work with.

Reflection: This semester I spent a lot more time working with someone on designing then I thought I was going to, which put my teamwork skills to the test a few times. I think overall though, there was not much miscommunication, most of the time the ideas for design were interpretted well and sections of time I spent working back and forth on designs were good notes of the semester. Definitely would say there was an improvement in a communication for design choices.

Critical & Creative Thinking - Inquiry & Analysis

Goal: Since my job title includes both development and also data analysis, I have a goal this semester to bridge the two and develop both skills through my ability to learn the information I need in a timely manner, and my analysis abilities for working on technical projects.

Action Plan: I've already had a few opportunities to improve my inquiry and analysis skills through varying tasks that I've had to complete research for or involved something I did not necessarily know how to accomplish. I feel that I will continue to have more opportunities like this throughout this term that will allow me to learn more and grow in my skills.

Measure of Success: I will consider success in my growth of my inquiry and analysis abilities if I continue to be able to accomplish all the tasks assigned to me that require types of development I have to research first, and if I make useful notes of the information I'm given.

Reflection: This semester I spent a good bit of my time researching what I could and could not do in both the hard coding of Bootstrap and then designing and coding functions for WordPress. I would say that the time I spent researching never felt wasted, information that I found and skills I learned from each time I did some searching turned into direct knowledge that I used for the remaining term developing on the web pages.

Job Description

During this work term I had the opportunity to work on one main project with a few side activities and grow my current skills, as well as develop a few new ones on the job.

Main Project

Web Guide Site Password: cpadev
UI Kit Site Password: cpadev

My main project for the University's Communications team started with some research on other University Web Guide pages, determining strengths and flaws with each page, and taking note of the information delivered to the user. From there I began an overhall of the University of Guelph's Web Guide and UI Kit pages. I changed the layout and presentation of the UI Kit, adding new information, sample descriptions, separating different topics to new pages. I even created some new functionality in the javascipt section of the webpage. After the Web Guide and UI Kit were where we wanted it, I took on the task of learning how to use WordPress and then the process of porting the progress on the git repository onto WordPress.

New Skills

During school my web development focused more on the interaction between languages, and the handling of changing information to create a dynamic webpage. During this work term I had the chance to learn a new language, PHP, as well as learn more about different aspects of web development and other web development tools such as Bootstrap and SCSS. Working on the UI Kit sections in particular got me much more familiar with how to create a webpage that is both informative and well designed for the user, but also organised and easy to understand for others looking for simple tools for their pages. Learning how to use WordPress was also a great experience, learning a new way to design and develop a web page that isn't directly in the code.

Conclusions

Overall my experiences with the University of Guelph Communications and Public Affairs Team was an incredibly positive one. My skills and knowledge in both web development and web page designing have had good progress and I feel much more comfortable with web development work now, and even learning to be adaptable with learning new development procedures through learning WordPress was a great experience. Overall I am very excited to see where my career in development will take me next, and I hope I will learn just as much as I did in this position.
