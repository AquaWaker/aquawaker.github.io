S23 Work Term Report

Brayden Pritchard

Employer: Magnet Forensics

Position: Software Developer

Introduction

This webpage is a collection of reflections and summaries of my fourth and final co-op work term that was with Magnet Forensics as a Software Developer over the summer 2023 semester. Presented is information pertaining to my employer, different learning goals I had, and summaries of what I worked on and skills I learned.

Employer Information

This past summer I spent my last co-op term working for Magnet Forensics, specifically on their Review team. The Review product is a web based application that allows forensics teams to dive deeper into information found on various electronic devices collected through Magnet's other applications. Review is a product meant for collaboration so it includes features for various users to share comments, and tag various pieces of evidence for future investigation.

Learning Goals

Communicating - Written communication

Goal: My first goal for this last co-op term is to continue working on developing my ability to share my thoughts and ideas better through written communication as I feel this is one of my weak points.

Action Plan: Between working with others on code testing, working on my own tickets where I need help, and doing QA work, I will have plenty of opportunities to train myself in learning better ways to communicate ideas to people.

Measure of Success: I want to be able to concisely share the information that the other person needs to know, as the work term progresses I will use the opportunities given to me to keep working on my wording and if I see that I need to do less calls to express my ideas and don't need to reword what I say as often I will call that a success.

Reflection: This semester was probably the most I've worked on projects with other team members directly, either working together, through back and forth advice and guidance or through peer reviews. This led to many opportunities for written communications between the various tasks. By the end of the semester I felt I had successfully learned a lot of the workplace language and understood what I was working on well enough to concisely convey the information I had to share when requesting help. I feel like a lot of the understanding I've been building up throughout the last few coop jobs really came together on this last one and I didn't struggle with having to repeat myself.

Critical & Creative Thinking - Inquiry and Analysis

Goal: My second goal for this co-op term is to continue to grow in my ability to be able to study code written by others and be able to glean what the code does and be able to ask relevant questions in hopes of deepening my understanding of the product and other languages.

Action Plan: By participating in code reviews and QA work, I get the chance to look into other people's code and test it. I want to use this as an opportunity to challenge myself to be active in conversations about other people's work and learn from what others are working on.

Measure of Success: By the end of the semester I want to participate more and more in the code reviews, asking more insightful questions rather than basic questions about the code and eventually maybe making suggestions and insights myself to help other members of the team.

Reflection: This was an interesting goal, because a little past midway through the semester I started feeling comfortable enough with my knowledge of the product and the languages to start asking questions about other tickets, which was my goal. However, once I started really participating we moved into a testing phase and then our attention was shifted to a completely different part of the product so I had to build up some of the knowledge again in the last three weeks of the work term. Overall I'm excited that I got to participate in some code reviews and debriefing of tickets and am happy with the level of inquiry I got to.

Critical & Creative Thinking - Problem Solving

Goal: This being my last co-op work term and with technologies I haven't worked with yet, I feel like this is the perfect opportunity to both show my progress and grow my abilities to adapt, learn, and solve issues in new languages.

Action Plan: This will be something that naturally happens throughout the semester but I have been taking greater care to push myself to try things I'm unfamiliar with rather than constantly stick to what I know.

Measure of Success: If by the end of the summer I have had the opportunity and successfully completed work in various languages and with new technologies, where I feel I could go back and complete work with the same things and be able to work more efficiently, I will feel that I have succeed in progressing my abilities to learn new ways to solve problems.

Reflection: I almost exactly followed my measure of success section. I spent a good half of the year learning about many different technologies, various languages they used, and different sections of the product. Then I began picking up tickets in areas I had previously worked on and I could tell immediately working on them that they were going quicker due to my new understanding. I was able to effectively solve new problems basing my understanding on previous tasks.

Job Description

Working with the tacos squad of the Review team, my main work consisted of backend development, fixing bugs and creating unit testing for both new features I made and existing ones that required more. I worked on many different types of deliverables during my time at Magnet Forensics. Some of which included fixing bugs created by new features such as making sure new variable types were deleted when cases were deleted, creating new automatic testing that runs on the application each day to make sure new bugs are not arising such as a unit test that runs the application and deletes evidence, then checks to see if the evidence is really gone. I also had the opportunity to work on some of the new features that are still in progress now. I even had the opportunity to work through QA testing of other team members' tasks, and be involved in various code reviews for my own code as well as other people's.

New Skills

This job gave me the opportunity to work in C# for the first time, and having done a lot of PHP and C work between school and other jobs I felt very comfortable with it very quickly. I had a few issues with some of the classes included in libraries we had but it made for a great teachable moment with one of my other coworkers to go through it. I also got plenty of learning experience in various unit testing. There were multiple different portions of the code that required unit testing throughout my time at Magnet, and I got lots of practice learning different ways of creating tests, even getting to use Cypress for some of them.

Conclusions

As my final co-op work term, I can confidently say that this was all I could have hoped for in a job experience. Working with a great team of developers who were a well of knowledge to tap into, and developing on various differing areas of the product, I developed strong technical and communication skills throughout this term. I am especially glad I got to work on so much unit testing because I felt it was a skill I had not been able to work on enough between school projects and other jobs I have had so far. Now as I move into my last year of school I feel confident with the skills I have developed and learned from all of my co-op jobs, and I am excited to be able to begin my career.

Acknowledgements

From Noah being available to help me with any questions I had, to Garrett giving me deeper dives into the code. And there were plenty of other team members that helped me out too, so I'm thankful to all of them for helping me learn so much during this work term.
