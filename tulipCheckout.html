W23 Work Term Report

Brayden Pritchard

Employer: Tulip Retail

Position: Software Developer

Introduction

This webpage is a collection of reflections and summaries of my third co-op work term that was with Tulip Retail as a Backend Developer over the Winter 2023 semester. Presented is information pertaining to my employer, different learning goals I had, and summaries of what I worked on and skills I learned.

Employer Information

Over the winter I had the exciting opportunity to work with Tulip Retail for a second time, specifically with their Checkout team. During my time there, I developed code for their Point of Sale application meant for sales associates, creating new features, fixing various bugs, and working on the backend API. Point of Sale is only the most recent of Tulip's endeavors of ecommerce products as they also assist companies with Clienteling.

Learning Goals

Professional & Ethical Behaviour - Teamwork

Goal: My first goal for this co-op term is to continue growing my ability to work as part of a team unit and build up good communication skills for working through problems with people.

Action Plan: Through working on different bugs and features, I will challenge myself to learn how to bring up issues I'm having and how to articulate where I'm at with tasks. This also includes working alongside non-developers on issues that I need other's input for.

Measure of Success: If my tasks are completed in relatively good time and I don't struggle with explaining myself to people when I need help with issues or need input I'll consider that a success

Reflection: Over the course of the semester I had many opportunities to communicate with my other team members over different topics such as looking for advice on an issue, sharing how to test my changes in detail and getting information from others about code related to what I was working on. This required both professional language for those I did not quite know as well in the company as well as strong communication for teamwork related to collaborating on tasks and I got to do exactly this. There was one particular task that I worked on that required me to work alongside a non-developer on the team, as well as the front end dev to make a change. It took some teamwork to figure out how I could make my backend changes line up with the front end's changes in such a way that solved the teams ask fors on the task.

Communicating - Written communication

Goal: My second goal is tied very closely to the first one, which is growing my skills in communication. Specifically, written communication about what I'm working on, and articulating my progress

Action Plan: Be specific and concise when messaging with others about the different tasks I'm working on. As well as being informative when I put merge requests up with work I've done, explaining what I did.

Measure of Success: If my conversations online go smoothly and I don't get a lot of clarifying questions as the semester goes on I'll consider that a success in growing my written communication skills.

Reflection: As I said before in the Teamwork reflection, there was plenty of opportunities to work on my written communication skills and often that was the main communication channel for many questions, from me, or others about my tasks. Going off of my measure of success specifically, I got a lot better at giving testing paths as the semester went on, making sure that anyone that was going to test the changes could follow exactly what needed to be done and know what the expected results should be. Overall, there was a clear improvement in my written communications as the semester went, noticeably getting less clarification questions from others about my messages and code summaries which I consider a great success.

Critical & Creative Thinking - Problem Solving

Goal: My third goal is a relatively simple one considering my field but I want to continue my skills in being about to problem solve, and come up with solutions on my own or research based on what I know to find solutions

Action Plan: This will come up with every issue I face during the work term, basically I want to be able come up with better solutions on my own and when I can't, I want to grow my skills in knowing what questions to ask to get closer to the solution of my problem, whether that be messaging someone with specific questions, or using Google for help.

Measure of Success: This comes down to how well I feel I complete the different tasks I'm given. So working hard to solve more difficult tasks as the semester progresses, or being able to complete tasks related to things I'm not used to working with. I want to learn more about technologies I'm not used to, and be more comfortable with the ones I have used.

Reflection: This one is fairly simple compared to the last two, I feel like as the semester went on I was able to take on harder, more impactful tasks. Needing less guidence to find where I needed to make my changes, and also was able to keep up with technical discussion with other developers better. Definitely feel more comfortable with Javascript and PHP then I did before, and I know a few more coding techniques!

Job Description

My position on the checkout team involved mainly backend development, with a small handful of changes to the frontend to complement backend changes. My various deliverables included tasks such as deprecating code that was no longer needed, bugs involving inventory management such as items appearing in stores they should not appear in or items unable to be added to empty lists, and working on error messages made from tax calculation errors that were being silenced. During my tasks I interacted with other members of the team for some collaboration on changes, and lots of feedback on my various changes.

New Skills

This is my third position that has been primarily PHP focused code, and even though I have worked with it a lot now, there were plenty of new skills I learned. The main thing I feel stronger in is the interaction of various different PHP files passing information and functions for use. One of the other specific topics I learned was some Regex in PHP. Also I worked in Javascript for a few of the changes, reinforcing my core skills in the language. Overall I learned a great deal reading existing code and adding new features around it.

Conclusions

Being the second time I've worked for a team at Tulip Retail, this work term allowed me to compare how various developer teams operate. Being very close to the clients before, my last position we did not really have build phases where we focused on very specific development paths as a team, but instead we worked task by task, working towards whatever the clients needed at the time. Following the build phase driven development path with the Checkout Team was a great learning experience, we worked on features for all clients, but also fixed issues affecting multiple companies signing on. I liked the drive to specific goals, working with other team members on larger deliverables. All the experiences both in the development itself, and the team aspect of the job have been valuable for my steps towards my career, and I'm excited for how it will help me moving onto my last co-op job before finishing school.

Acknowledgements

The whole checkout team was super helpful to me, offering me plenty of advice and guidance throughout the work term. Specifically my manager Mathieu, and my coworkers Dan and Michael. There were a lot of kind words from many other team members too, and I greatly appreciate the experience I gained with the team.
